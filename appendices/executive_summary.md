Executive Summary: The Unified AI Red Team Framework (2026)
Strategic Overview for Stakeholders and Decision Makers

The Problem: The "Permissive Default" Crisis
As organizations rush to integrate Agentic AI and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), they are inheriting a massive, unmapped attack surface. Traditional security treats AI as a "Black Box," but in 2026, the threats are Stateful, Temporal, and Boundary-crossing. Small businesses are particularly vulnerable to "silent" compromises that lead to data exfiltration and economic exhaustion.

The Solution: The State + Time + Boundary Model
The Unified AI Red Team Framework (by SKITZOSystems) moves beyond simple prompt injection. It provides a standardized taxonomy of 25 tactics to harden AI systems at the architectural level.

STATE: Hardening the AI’s memory and knowledge retrieval (RAG) against poisoning.

TIME: Preventing delayed triggers and "Semantic Drift" where a model’s behavior degrades over a session.

BOUNDARY: Securing the handoffs between users, agents, and external tools.

Business Impact & ROI
Risk Mitigation: Prevents high-cost "Economic DoS" and catastrophic "Goal Hijacking."

Compliance Readiness: Aligns internal AI development with emerging standards like NIST AI RMF and OWASP.

Trust as a Feature: Enables businesses to market their AI implementations as "Hardened" and "Secure," providing a competitive edge in a crowded market.

Implementation Roadmap
Tier 1: Immediate "Least Privilege" and Cost-Ceiling controls.

Tier 2: Schema enforcement and multi-agent intent validation.

Tier 3/4: Continuous adversarial testing and "Gatekeeper" monitoring architectures.
